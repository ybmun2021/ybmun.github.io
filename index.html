Welcome to YBMUN V!

Dear Delegates, Advisors, Future diplomats, and Leaders,

As the Secretary‑General, it is with utmost honour that I invite you to the fifth chapter of Yuvabharathi Model United Nations, taking place on the 23rd and 24th of August 2025, at Yuvabharathi Public School. I am incredibly delighted to serve as the Secretary-General of a conference that was born out of passion and purpose, where each session, each debate, and each resolution is a tribute to the dedication and spirit of its participants and organisers.

Over the two days, YBMUN will encourage delegates to engage and think critically in alignment with YBMUN V's theme, "Peace in the Digital Age".

YBMUN began as an intraschool competition, and we step into our fifth chapter this year, expanding our shared passion for debate, diplomacy, and decorum. This evolution stands as a testament to the shared passion and dedication of every delegate, faculty advisor, and member of our Secretariat. Today, YBMUN shines as a beacon of hope and unity, serving as a platform for young minds to engage with the world's most pressing issues.

As someone who's been seated at both the sides of the table, both as a delegate and an Executive Board Member, I can confidently say that MUNs are not just about speeches, it's where we as delegates shed our differences and limitations, it is where we embrace the art of negotiation, the discipline of research, and the courage to voice out our opinions. It is where we learn to listen as keenly as we speak, try to understand others ' viewpoints before seeking to be understood, and to find common ground amidst plural perspectives.

As Secretary‑General, I'm committed to ensuring that this conference remains a space where every thought holds value, where every delegate is heard, and every discussion builds bridges–not walls.

Once again, on behalf of the YBMUN team, it is my absolute honour to extend a heartfelt and resounding welcome to every one of you to YBMUN V.

May diplomacy guide your steps, and may your ideas shape our shared future!

 

Sincerely,

Kavya S A

Secretary-General
